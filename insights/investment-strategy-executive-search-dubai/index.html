Investment and Strategy Executive Search in Dubai: Placing Leaders Who Shape Decisions

Confidential search for private equity, sovereign fund, asset management and strategy leadership in the GCC

The Gulf has become one of the world's most significant centres of capital. Sovereign wealth funds, family offices, private equity firms, and regional asset managers are deploying capital at a scale and pace that is reshaping global investment markets. Behind that capital are the leadership teams responsible for deploying it wisely.

Finding those leaders — and attracting them — requires a different kind of search.

The investment and strategy talent market in the GCC

Senior investment and strategy professionals in the GCC operate in a distinctive environment. Mandates are large, stakeholder expectations are high, and the cultural intelligence required to operate effectively across regional relationships is significant. International experience is often valued, but it must be combined with an understanding of how the Gulf market actually works.

The candidate pool for these roles is international but concentrated. The executives best suited to senior investment and strategy positions in the GCC are often based in London, New York, Singapore, or Zurich — or already operating within the region at firms that would prefer not to see them leave.

Roles we place in investment and strategy

  • Chief Investment Officer (CIO)
  • Head of Private Equity / Private Credit
  • Director of Investments
  • Head of Corporate Strategy / Group Strategy
  • Chief Strategy Officer (CSO)
  • Head of Portfolio Management
  • Head of M&A and Transactions
  • Managing Director, Asset Management

What makes search in this space work

Investment and strategy mandates are rarely won through advertising. The professionals who hold or aspire to hold these positions are selective, well-advised, and often engaged with multiple opportunities simultaneously.

Success depends on two things: reach and credibility. The ability to open conversations with high-calibre, passive candidates — and to do so in a way that represents the opportunity compellingly and protects the client's confidentiality throughout.
